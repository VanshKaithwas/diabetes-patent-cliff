Why I made this

In IP class we covered how pharmaceutical patents work — companies get around 20 years of exclusivity on a drug. After that, generic manufacturers can copy it and sell it way cheaper. The original company loses most of its revenue really fast. This is called a patent cliff.

I wanted to see which diabetes drugs were actually facing this right now. I picked diabetes because it's one of the biggest drug markets globally. And I focused on Boehringer Ingelheim specifically because they're a major German pharma company — I'm applying to TUM so understanding German companies felt relevant.

The tools I used — Python, Pandas, SQL — are all from my Class 12 IT subject. I just applied them to this instead of the usual school practicals.

What I found

Boehringer Ingelheim has three diabetes drugs expiring in 2025 — Jardiance, Synjardy, and Trajenta. That's $7B in one year facing generic competition. I didn't expect that much concentrated in a single year for one company.

2026 is actually the worst year overall — Farxiga, Toujeo and others expire then, putting over $15B at risk at once. Novo Nordisk on the other hand is in a pretty safe position, most of their big drugs don't expire until 2031+.

I also did a quick German vs non-German comparison. German companies (basically just Boehringer Ingelheim here) hold about 14% of the tracked revenue, almost all from their SGLT2 inhibitor drugs.
